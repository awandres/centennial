Prepared for the Next Century

The explosive growth of the industry in China, the Middle East, and other developing portions of the world during this decade demonstrate the industry’s global appeal. The debut of The Wizarding World of Harry Potter at Universal’s Islands of Adventure in Orlando ushered in the age of immersive lands dedicated to a single story/intellectual property (IP), which drove billions of dollars in new investments among major theme park resorts around the world. At the other end of the spectrum, major operators opened IP-driven, smaller-scale attractions throughout the globe to appeal to time-pressed consumers. Technology continued to play an increasing role in attractions, enhancing not only their entertainment value but safety. It has truly become a global industry with numerous attractions operators crossing international borders and suppliers selling seamlessly throughout the world.

2010 | In September, the association established the IAAPA Asia-Pacific regional office in Hong Kong with travel, hospitality, and trade show veteran Andrew Lee as executive director.

The IAAPA Foundation was formed as a separate nonprofit corporation to fund the development of education courses and industry information.

IAAPA expanded its Safety Institutes program with the Safety Roadshow, featuring nine total sessions at industry conferences and exhibitions in Asia, the Middle East, Latin America, and the United States.

The IAAPA Brass Ring Awards expanded in scale to be the main awards ceremony at IAAPA Attractions Expo. In addition to its traditional focus on marketing, it incorporated the Big E entertainment and Spirit of Excellence human resources awards programs, along with the Top FEC, retail excellence, best new products, and exhibit awards.

Bob Rippy of North Carolina’s Jungle Rapids became the first chairman of the board from the FEC constituency.

2011 | Attractions industry veteran James “Chip” Cleary took over as president and CEO, replacing Charlie Bray.

The IAAPA Certification program, designed to elevate professional standards of the industry through a combination of classroom work and on-the-job experience, launched with three levels of certification—Manager, Professional, and Executive.

The first IAAPA Leadership Conference was held in San Diego. A re-imagining of the former IAAPA Summer Meeting, the event is designed to give industry members a chance to network as they gain behind-the-scenes access to attractions and learn from other leaders.

The IAAPA Institute for Executive Education was re-launched with academic partner San Diego State University, whose resources helped the association replicate this offering elsewhere around the world.

2012 | At Asian Attractions Expo in June, the association began implementing its new Intellectual Property program to assist exhibiting companies in protecting their concepts and products. It was also utilized at Euro Attractions Show and IAAPA Attractions Expo and remains in place today. The process helped dozens of manufacturers and suppliers address show-floor examples of patent, trademark, and copyright infringement. Sixty-five investigations were launched the first year in partnership with law firm Arent Fox LLP.

The association adopted its first multiyear Strategic Plan since 2007, utilizing a new three-year time horizon (2013-2015) and basing it on an unprecedented mix of organizational material and data. The resulting goals emphasized safety certification, member relationships,long-term sustainability, and unified governance.

In an effort to reach members closer to their home facilities, IAAPA organized regional events such as a Tools of the Trade tour of three Pennsylvania parks and a Regional Networking Event at Medieval Times in Toronto.

John McReynolds became the association’s final third vice chair, as the IAAPA Board of Directors eliminated the position to streamline the officer progression process.

2013 | Following a six-month transition process, Paul Noland, who worked at Marriott and with Walt Disney Parks and Resorts, became the association’s next president and CEO, succeeding Chip Cleary, who returned to a leadership position in the industry.

Mario Mamon of Enchanted Kingdom in the Philippines became the first board chair from the Asia-Pacific region.

The IAAPA Institute for Attractions Managers becomes a global program with classes offered in Dubai, the United States, Singapore, Colombia, and France.

The IAAPA Foundation released its first project, a study of the “The Economic Impact of the U.S. Attractions Industry.” The study included not only a 56-page report that presented information on the impact of the attractions industry nationally, by state, and by attraction type, but also included an online searchable database calculator.

The FEC Summit, an educational and networking event for FEC professionals, debuted in Phoenix, Arizona. From this point, IAAPA has continued to offer five annual Signature Events: the three Expos, IAAPA Leadership Conference, and IAAPA FEC Summit.

2014 | IAAPA launched a new governance structure—One World, One IAAPA—establishing a North American regional office in Orlando and providing a greater role for committees in the development of the strategic plan, business plan, and budgeting.

2015 | IAAPA celebrated 20 years of its partnership with Give Kids The World.

2016 | The association expanded its government relations capacity by launching a state advocacy program, including retaining lobbyists in states considering legislation that would impact the industry, disseminating state-level regulatory tracking, and providing government relations resources to members.

In September, IAAPA announced it would relocate its global headquarters from Alexandria, Virginia, to Orlando. The Central Florida location would make it easier for IAAPA members from around the world to connect with one another and engage with IAAPA’s professional staff when they travel to the region to meet with industry suppliers and visit attractions.

The IAAPA Foundation relaunched with a new mission focusing on developing the next generation of leaders through partnerships with the University of Central Florida Rosen College of Hospitality Management and JA Worldwide.

The agreement to hold IAAPA Attractions Expo at the Orange County Convention Center was extended through 2030.

2017 | IAAPA officially relocated its global headquarters team to Orlando, moving into a temporary office space near the Orange County Convention Center. Plans for building a permanent headquarters are taking shape, with a debut set for 2019. The seven-acre site was acquired for the association’s new global headquarters, within easy driving distance from the Orlando International Airport. Designs released for the new 21,375-square-foot building showcase how the new facility will serve the association and its members innew ways, with meeting space for members when doing business in Orlando.

2018 | The IAAPA Leadership Conference travels to Asia for the first time with visits to Osaka and Tokyo, Japan.
