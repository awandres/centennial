IAAPA ORAL HISTORY PROJECT

The mission of the IAAPA Oral History Project is to honor the attractions pioneers, trailblazers, and risk-takers, the people whose vision, passion, and persistence built a great industry. In so doing, we hope to educate those who currently carry the torch and inspire the next generation of global attractions industry leaders. Over the year, visit this page to hear the IAAPA story in their own words.

John Graff

During his 20 years of service with IAAPA, John Graff transformed the mostly United States-based organization of the early 1980s into a respected global association.

Alberto Zamperla

Since the 1960s, Zamperla SpA has manufactured some of the attractions industry's most iconic rides. President and CEO Alberto Zamperla shares his company's history and the importance of the IAAPA trade shows to a diversifying market.

Rene Aziz

Rene Aziz opened his first park in 1983 with only one ride. Three years later, he joined IAAPA, knowing few park operators outside Mexico. By 2000, he was elected IAAPA Chairman of the Board, the first chairman from Latin America.

Gina Romano

Gina Romano was the first woman to chair the IAAPA Board of Directors. She grew up in the industry, working at her family's attraction, Nellie Bly Amusement Park in Brooklyn, New York. Romano led the way in showing how women should be part of the growing international organization.

Tony Sze

Chimelong’s Tony Sze has seen the amusement park segment flourish in China, and across Asia Pacific. He has served as a member on the IAAPA Board of Directors and shares his insight into the region’s booming industry.

Bob Masterson

During his 36 years at Ripley’s Entertainment, Bob Masterson took a collection of small museums filled with oddities and built an attraction chain respected around the world. He served as the first chairman from a small attraction.

Carl Hughes

While president of Pennsylvania’s Kennywood amusement park, Carl Hughes was elected IAAPA President (now chairman) of the IAAPA Board of Directors—the first non-owner to hold such a major role in the association.

Tim O'Brien

Tim O'Brien is a long-time attractions industry journalist and IAAPA Hall of Fame recipient. He talks about the evolution of the attractions industry.

Mario Mamon

When Mario Mamon and his family were making plans to build their own theme park in the Philippines, their first stop was the IAAPA convention and trade show. Mamon would go on to become the first IAAPA chairman from Asia-Pacific.

Ricardo Castillo Sinibaldi

Ricardo Castillo Sinibaldi has built five amusement parks and several high quality hotels in his native country of Guatemala over the past 50 years. He founded the Institute for the Recreation of the Workers of the Guatamalan Private Sector (IRTRA) and is a recipient of the IAAPA Lifetime Service Award.

Marcelo Gutglas

Marcelo Gutglas founded the first amusement park in Brazil in the 1970s. He went on to launch Hopi Hari, another successful amusement park, and the Playland chain of family entertainment centers, which can now be found throughout Latin America. He also founded two industry associations: ADIBRA and SINDEPAT.

Phil Lindsey

Phil Lindsey started his career in the theme park industry, but soon found a lifelong calling in museums and science centers. He discusses the responsibilities attractions have to their communities and the commonalities across the industry.

Peter Herschend

Peter Herschend founded Herschend Family Entertainment with his brother Jack and talks about the origins of the company.

Dennis Spiegel

Dennis Speigel is president of International Theme Park Services and has been an active IAAPA member for decades. He played an integral role in the openings of Kings Island and Kings Dominion among other groundbreaking attractions.

Jack Herschend

Jack Herschend founded Herschend Family Entertainment with his brother Peter. The company owns and operates entertainment and attractions properties across 23 states and is still privately owned.

Bob Minick

Founder of Minick Associates, Bob Minick has had a hand in the opening and design of some of North America's most beloved theme parks.

Bo Kinntorph

Bo Kinntorph, former CEO of Liseberg, was the first IAAPA chair from outside the United States and played a key role in internationalizing the association.

Ted Molter

Ted Molter has had a long career in the industry focusing on animals in human care. He talks about the importance of these attractions and their benefit to humankind.

Marty Skylar

Playing a vital role in the creation of Walt Disney Parks and Resorts, Marty Sklar is an industry luminary who has had an incredible impact on IAAPA and the industry worldwide.

Gary Story

Industry legend Gary Story formerly served as president and chairman of Six Flags Corp. and founded Premier Parks, LLC.

Richard Sherman

IAAPA Hall of Fame Member Richard Sherman composed some of the industry's most familiar tunes and demonstrated the power of music on the attractions experience.
